Executive Summary & Objectives

The Phase 1 CRM implementation mandates the creation of a centralized, structurally rigid sales environment to eliminate lead leakage, enforce accountability, and establish automated operational rules.

⚠️

Current Problems

  • 🔴
    Data Leakage & Fragmentation Leads are currently scattered across Excel sheets, WhatsApp messages, and unmonitored emails.
  • 🔴
    No Accountability Framework Lack of clear assignment logic, ownership clarity, and SLA enforcement for sales follow-ups.
  • 🔴
    Compromised Data Integrity Severe duplicate client issues exist due to the absence of a standardized database and merging rules.
🎯

Business Goals

  • 🟢
    Absolute Centralization Unify all incoming leads, clients, and transactional data into a single, secure, and auditable system.
  • 🟢
    Automated Accountability (SLAs) Establish systemic rules that automatically flag neglected leads and notify management of pipeline stagnation.
  • 🟢
    Real-Time Business Intelligence Enable management to view live KPI dashboards filtered dynamically by branch, team, or individual rep.

1. Roles & Stakeholders Matrix

Defining the actors within the system and their primary operational objectives.

⚙️

Administrator

Technical Control

Goal: Technical configuration, user management, and system maintenance.

Scope: Absolute access to all modules, settings, and destructive actions.

👁️

Manager (Global)

Executive Oversight

Goal: View and monitor everything across the entire company without accidentally altering data.

Scope: Has "View All" permissions across every single module, branch, and dashboard. Cannot edit, create, or delete records.

📊

Sales Manager

Branch Operations

Goal: Team oversight, workload balancing, and SLA enforcement.

Scope: Hierarchical view of the assigned branch team. Can re-assign leads and view team KPIs.

💼

Sales Representative

Pipeline Execution

Goal: Conversion of assigned leads efficiently through the pipeline.

Scope: Siloed view. Only sees owned/assigned records. Logs activities and manages own tasks.

♻️

After-Sales Specialist

Client Retention

Goal: Proactive renewal management before certificate expiry.

Scope: Access to "Won" deals, expiry trackers, and renewal workflows.

💰

Finance Department

Revenue Control

Goal: Accurate revenue forecasting and quotation standardization.

Scope: Access to quotes, scheme-specific pricing rules, and discount approvals.

2

Deals / Opportunities Module

The financial tracking core of the CRM.

Generated exclusively by converting a Lead or manually creating a Renewal. Deals must strictly link to the defined relational entities below.

🤝
Relational Link 1 Must link to the Client/Broker (Payer).
🏢
Relational Link 2 Must link to the Facility (Manufacturer).
💵
Financial Core Tracks Stage, Expected Revenue, and Close Date.
3

Sales: Accounts / Clients

The billing entities and communication hubs. Contains the critical "Broker Toggle" to differentiate third-party agencies from direct manufacturers.

Key Concept: One Account (Broker) can manage multiple Facilities.
4

Sales: Contacts / Facilities

The physical manufacturing locations or brands receiving the actual certificate. They do not hold billing information.

Key Concept: A Facility must always inherit from the overarching Company structure but is treated as a separate node in Deals.
5

Tasks / Follow-ups

The accountability tracker. Users schedule calls, meetings, or emails here. Completion of a task updates the `Last_Activity_Date` on the parent Deal, resetting the SLA 96-hour timer.

6

Dashboards

Visual reporting tools providing real-time BI. Data visibility inside the dashboard is dynamically restricted based on the User Roles matrix (Module 7).
